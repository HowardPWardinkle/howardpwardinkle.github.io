## Idea

This is the first in a three-card series about the American space program from the 1960s. I grew up in that era and all the kids were wild about space. I had a space helmet (which later was converted into a snowman's head for Halloween), and this metal suit case of space toys by Marx.

![Space toys](/images/2606/toy.png){: width="{{ site.imageWidthTwoThird }}" }

The era in which we live now is also exciting. There are two space stations (International Space Station and Tiangong, the Chinese one.), other countries and companies sending landers to the moon. Multiple rovers on Mars. We've brought back samples from asteriods! My personal favorites are the two [Voyager spacecrafts](https://science.nasa.gov/mission/voyager/where-are-voyager-1-and-voyager-2-now/). They have been been operating for nearly 50 years, and now are one light-day away from Earth (over 15 billion miles travelling over 69,000 miles per hour).

My only other space-related card was the [April 2021](/2021/03/28/mars-rover.html) one featuring the latest Mars rover, Perseverance, and its helicopter, Ingenuity.

## Design

The design is a fairly simple one. The space capsule is a parallelogram. The banner at the top uses a new mechanism for me, the paper spring. This is some paper folded in a zig-zag pattern that will, hopefully, spring up when the card is opened. Here's a little test I did to see which way the grain of the paper should go. The center one is against the grain and doesn't spring up as well as the other two, which have folds parallel to the grain.

![spring](/images/2606/spring.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthTwoThird }}" }
*The spring*{: .image-caption}

And here are the springs under the banner.

![spring in action](/images/2606/spring-2.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthTwoThird }}" }
*The spring in action*{: .image-caption}

The lower left has a second pop-up with a rocket. The cover has the Project Mercury logo.  The rocket is an acute angle V-fold, about as large as I could make it. I was going to make the rockets in each of the series to the same scale, but the Atlas rocket is about the third of the size of the Saturn V. I tried paper and cardstock versions, but the paper opened better. I very lightly scored the main fold to reduce the stress and make it fully open.

![rocket](/images/2606/rocket.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthTwoThird }}" }
*Rocket*{: .image-caption}

As usual with V-fold that life backwards, this one tilts back slightly. If it was configured to open vertically, it would tend to lean forward a bit.

![rocket tilted](/images/2606/tilted.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthTwoThird }}" }
*Tilting backwards*{: .image-caption}

To hold the nested pop-up closed, I used the same method from the [November 2025](/2025/10/29/november.html) card, that uses a small triangular tab.

![Corner](/images/2606/corner.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthTwoThird }}" }
*Setting the corner*{: .image-caption}

Another nice card with some simple mechanisms. Since this is a series, you will see this repeated the next two months.
