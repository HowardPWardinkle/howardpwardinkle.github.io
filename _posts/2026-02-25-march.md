## Idea

Exciting news in the Wardinkle clan! Howard Jr., is now a proud homeowner. To celebrate, I thought it would be fun to do a card with his new home on it.

I enjoy creating Origami Architecture (OA) cards, which are one-piece pop-ups that are nested parallel folds. There are many books on them, some of which I have listed [here](/books.html#origami-architecture). In the past I have made two of my own designs: Independence Hall, and then one from a painting of my previous residence.

## Design

As I mentioned above, OA cards are one-piece, but with the porch overhang, I had to forgo making a true one-piece card. Instead, this is two pieces, one for the main house and one for the porch.

I did one small sketch, then went to the computre to design the card. Here are three full-size sketches of the card. The mechanism has minor changes between them. The main differences are the coloring. I had three options: 1) All white, which is rather traditional for OA cards, 2) Red trim only and 3) Full color house.

![sketches](/images/2603/sketches.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*Three sketches with different color options*{: .image-caption}

I toyed with coloring the entire card with grass and trees, but thought that'd take away from the house, so I decided on just coloring the house. I used the {{ site.cameo }} to cut out the sketches, but the scan-and-cut feature is only accurate to about 1mm, which wont for this case where the cuts must be exactly on the edges of the printed areas. That meant I could only print it out and then cut everything by hand. The print out gave me nearly all of the cut lines. There are a few around the porch and roof that were not. To solve that, I printed very thin line in light gray for cut lines, which would be invisible in the final card.

> Buildings with sloped roofs require a different pop-up technique. Notice in the Independence Hall pop-up that there a slots in the back of the card to accept the sloping roofs. They cant be attached to the back since they're at a angle. Instead as the card is closed they push through the back of the card. We'll see details of that for this card below.

Here's the house print out cut and folded. It was pretty easy to cut by hand. The cut above the house is the slot for the sloped roof. (Notice none of the top edges are attached to the card. More on that later.)

![House](/images/2603/house.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*House with folds*{: .image-caption}

The porch roof will go over the center section. That also is the only part of the house that will be attached to the card base, thus lifting up the house. One thing I found from a sketch is that the white edges showed under the porch roof, so I colored those areas with colored pencil. The loose paper will be attached to the left and right sections of the roof to keep them in place, and I'll also attach the porch roof to it later.

![House](/images/2603/house-2.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*Coloring edges that may be seen*{: .image-caption}

For the card to work, the house must be attached to the back of the card. Since the house and porch roofs go from one end to another, the slot on the back for the sloped roof must go end-to-end. That means the house isn't attached to the back! Here's where I had to deviate from the one-piece model. I added a piece to the back for the center section to attach to, as shown in green below. Now, as the card is opened that center section raises up the house.

![House](/images/2603/house-3.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*House attached to the back*{: .image-caption}

Here's the side view showing the added support for the center section in green.

![House](/images/2603/side.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*Side view of the card*{: .image-caption}

The porch has to breaks the one-piece model, so I wasn't worried about the piece added for support. Here's the porch cut out and ready to be attached. This was the trickiest part to cut by hand since the porch supports are thin, and have the little triangles to cut out. Maybe it wasn't a good idea to use color.

![Porch](/images/2603/porch.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*Porch cut and ready to attach*{: .image-caption}

In the sketches, I had problems with the thin porch supports bending. To solve that, I glued extra pieces of card behind each one.

![Reinforced porch supports](/images/2603/porch-supports.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*Reinforced porch supports*{: .image-caption}

Since I'm no longer one-piece, why not add more with the chimney? This could have been cut out of the roof, which would have left a hole it it. This way it's prettier. The small slot in the roof is for the chimney. You can see the chimney is folded the way it will need to be attached under the roof.

![Chimney](/images/2603/chimney-1.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*Chimney*{: .image-caption}

Here it is about to be glued in place

![Chimney unglued](/images/2603/chimney-2.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidth }}" }
*Chimney about to be glued*{: .image-caption}

And here it is glued in place. It make a tiny parallelogram under the roof that lifs the chimney up as the card is opened.
