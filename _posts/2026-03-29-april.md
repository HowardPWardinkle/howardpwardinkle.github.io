## Idea

Mrs. Wardinkle and I run the [Dirty Spokes](https://dirtyspokes.com/) trail runs. This is their 20th year, and to celebrate I thought I'd create a card for it. The [April 2024](/2024/03/28/april.html) card celebrated my return to running after about 10 years of being away. Since returning I have run every one of the series events (12-14 per year).

The logo for Dirty Spoke is pretty neat, which could make an interesting card.

## Design

With the three distinct sections of the logo, I thought a tunnel book (or peep show) mechanism would work. Such a mechanism has multiple layers that give depth to the card. In this case there are only three, but the March 2014 card was a tunnel book with six layers.

![Old tunnel book](/images/2604/old-top.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthThird }}" }![Old tunnel book](/images/2604/old-front.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthThird }}" }
*March 2014 tunnel book top and front views*{: .image-caption}

The basic design of a tunnel book is like an accordian, with each layer attached to the wider parts of the pleats. The holes in layers get progressively smaller the the deeper you go into the book, giving the effect of depth and perspective.

Here's the top view showing the three layers of the tunnel book. The pieces on the left and right make the cover.

![top](/images/2604/top.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthHalf }}" }
*Top view showing the layers*{: .image-caption}

A straight on front view shows the layers, which look nearly flat in this photo.

![Front view](/images/2604/front.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthHalf }}" }
*Front view*{: .image-caption}

With fewer layers, this was much easier to build since there's a front, back and one middle layer. Since it was so simple I thought I'd make it a bit more interesting by making the gear in the middle layer be able to rotate. Usually when things rotate in a pop-up, they do it on a center pivot, but in this case the center is cut out. I thought that would be a problem, and it was. My solution was to create a circular slot in the gear piece and have tabs go through the slot and then the gear would rotate around the tabs.

My first try had four tabs that bent to the outside of the circular slot, which worked fine. Then I bent the card a bit and the ring just popped right out. I added four additional tabs next to each of the original tabs, but bent them to the inside. That kept the gear from popping off. It rotates, but would get stuck at a couple points. I found when gluing the paper ring down, if there was any glue near the tabs, it would get stuck. In the final version, I made sure to keep the glue away from the tabs and it rotates pretty well.

![Bottom view](/images/2604/bottom.jpg){: width="{{ site.imageWidthHalf }}" }
*Bottom view showing the gear and tabs*{: .image-caption}

One issue with the accordian and rotating gear is that the accoridian is very flexible left to right, which is the direction you push when rotating the gear. It works best when you hold the card by the middle layer, and then rotate the gear. It's not a great mechanism, but it does make a simple card more complex.

> Over the years, I've found that although complex cards are fun, they don't necessarily make a good card. A well-designed cards with a simple mechanism can be better than one with complex mechanisms.

As you see, when the card is fully open it doesn't stick out much, but it's the intermediate positions as the card is opened that stick out far too much to allow a loosely attached cover to work.

The final card works well, even if the cover is a bit odd being attached only at the leading edge. Lesson learned about sloping roofs in pop-up cards.
